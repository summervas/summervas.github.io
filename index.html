  • The end of summer often brings stress or even melancholy to most people
  • August has “all ingredients for a stew of blues,” said Joshua Klapow, a clinical psychologist and associate professor in the School of Public Health at The University of Alabama at Birmingham.
  • Most people wait for summer all year long, yet just 29% of Americans said summer was their favorite season in a 2020 CBS News poll.
  • 36% of consumers say they won’t be taking a holiday this summer – 14% say due to the level of their essential costs.
  • An online survey conducted by the National Recreation and Park Association (NRPA) found that 30% of Americans prefer to attend events during their summer holidays while 40% love going to the beach
  • There’s a great connection between weather and mood. “Warmer months do tend to have an impact on our overall happiness and well-being,” says Helene D. Moore, Psy.D., a psychiatrist at the Northwestern Medicine Osher Center for Integrative Medicine in Chicago.
